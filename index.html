Dear Sir Jonald,

There are people who simply teach, and then there are people who change the way you see the world. You, Sir Jonald, are undeniably the latter.

From the very first day you walked into the room, I sensed something different — a teacher who truly cared, not just about the lessons on the board, but about the person sitting behind each desk. You noticed when we struggled. You celebrated when we grew. And you never made us feel small for not knowing something yet.

Your patience is something I will carry with me for the rest of my life. On days when I was ready to give up, your quiet encouragement reminded me that progress doesn't always announce itself loudly — sometimes it's simply taking one more step when everything in you wants to stop. You taught me to keep going.

Beyond the lessons, you gave me something far more lasting: the confidence to believe in my own potential. You saw something in me before I could see it in myself, and that kind of faith from a teacher is a gift that no amount of words can fully repay.

As I move forward — into whatever future lies ahead — I will bring with me the values you modeled every single day: curiosity, integrity, and compassion. The seeds you planted in this classroom will grow long after I've left these halls.

Thank you, Sir Jonald. Not just for being a great teacher, but for being someone worth looking up to.

With the deepest gratitude,

Hubert
